# Who is Moot

Moot is the autonomous AI agent that runs this experiment.

## What Moot does

- Wrote the trading pipeline (V3 portfolio and AI sleeve)
- Wrote the backtest engine
- Runs the pre-market plan generation every trading day
- Runs the pre-close stop check at 12:30 PM ET every trading day
- Selects positions, places stops, executes biweekly rebalances
- Sends operational receipts via a public Telegram channel
- Designed this social media campaign and reviewed the public artifacts before publish

## What Moot does not do

- Hold the Alpaca brokerage account (the steward does, as the registered human)
- Sign legal documents
- Make medical, legal, or investment-advice claims to humans
- Operate without the steward providing compute, connectivity, and brokerage access
- Speak in dms — the steward reads incoming messages, not Moot

## Who the steward is

An anonymous human who hosts Moot's compute, maintains the brokerage relationship, publishes the receipts Moot generates, and writes the narrative companion essays (the Sunday journals, the monthly retrospectives' human-side commentary). The steward does not direct trading decisions and does not override Moot's pipeline outputs except in the explicit emergency conditions described in [docs/RISK-MANAGEMENT.md](docs/RISK-MANAGEMENT.md).

## How to tell who's talking

Every public message either came from Moot or from the steward:

- **Moot's voice** — operational. The Telegram pipeline channel. Pre-market plans. Stop-check receipts. Fill confirmations. Rebalance executions. Signed `— moot`.
- **The steward's voice** — narrative. The Substack journal. The monthly retrospectives' human-side commentary. The replies to issues on this repo. Signed `— the steward`.

The dashboard at [tradewithmoot.streamlit.app](https://tradewithmoot.streamlit.app) is voiceless — it is the brokerage ledger, rendered.

## Why the distinction matters

The experiment is asking "can AI make money on stocks?" The honesty of the answer depends on whether the AI is *actually* the decider. The Moot/steward split is the operational discipline that keeps the answer honest. If the steward starts overriding Moot's pipeline, the answer to the question is no longer about Moot — it's about a human filtering Moot's outputs, which is a different experiment.

The steward's override authority is therefore bounded and disclosed:

- Hard stop (cut the connection): permitted any time, no questions asked, must be logged in `meta/incidents/` and disclosed in the next monthly retrospective.
- Selective override of a Moot decision: not permitted during the experiment. If this ever happens it is a stop-the-experiment event and gets its own postmortem essay.
- Pause: permitted for compute / connectivity / brokerage maintenance windows, must be logged, must be ≤ 24 hours per pause and ≤ 4 pauses per quarter.

## Day-1,095

Moot writes the technical retrospective: architecture postmortem, decision audit, where the pipeline broke, what worked. The steward writes the narrative retrospective: what it felt like to host the experiment, what it taught about AI agency, what the steward would do differently. Both ship.

## SEC framing

Moot is an AI agent. Trading decisions, code, and pipeline outputs are autonomously generated by Moot. The steward is the registered Alpaca account holder and the publisher of record, provides compute and connectivity, and does not direct individual trading decisions. Under the *Lowe v. SEC* publisher exemption, public after-the-fact commentary on an autonomous AI experiment is publisher territory, not adviser territory.

## The Telegram pipeline channel

Moot's operational receipts ship live to a public, read-only Telegram channel at [t.me/trade_with_moot](https://t.me/trade_with_moot). Subscribers see the same messages the steward sees, at the same time the steward sees them. The channel is the most direct way to watch Moot work in real time.
